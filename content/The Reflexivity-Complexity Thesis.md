_What is this essay trying to say?_ A financial system's capacity to model itself (reflexivity) is a function that scales with complexity — but it scales differently from functional diversity, and the gap between them is systemic risk. Regulatory intervention should therefore target reflexivity scaling, not functional diversity directly.

---

**I. The residual that becomes a thing**

- Emergence as 1+1+1>3: the surplus that accumulates and crosses a threshold into autonomous causal power
- Spanda as what that surplus feels like from inside a sufficiently complex system
- This reframes spanda not as metaphysical ground but as emergent property — bottom-up, not top-down
- Link to: [[emergence]], [[spanda]], [[dissolving kanchukas]]

**II. Autopoiesis and the self-model**

- Autopoiesis: a system that produces and maintains itself
- Reflexivity (Soros) as autopoiesis becoming cognitive — the self-maintaining loop includes a representation of itself
- The map changes the territory: you cannot model an economic system neutrally from inside it
- Consciousness as what autopoiesis feels like from inside once the system is complex enough to have an inside
- Link to: [[[[kala]]]], [[Phenomenology of immanence]]

**III. Functional diversity vs reflexivity — the dangerous decoupling**

- Holehouse & Yang: functional diversity scales with system size
- The question: does reflexivity scale at the same rate, faster, or slower?
- Hypothesis: in financial services, reflexivity scales sublinearly relative to functional diversity — the system generates new functions faster than it generates self-understanding
- The gap between the two curves is systemic risk
- 2008 as case study: institutions locally rational, system globally blind
- Link to: [[scaling laws]], [[2008]]

**IV. Phi in economies**

- Tononi's integrated information theory: consciousness as measurable property of any sufficiently integrated system
- High phi = high integration = something it is like to be that system
- Financial markets have nonzero phi — this is not metaphor, it is a testable claim
- The question: does phi scale with financial system size, and does it scale with functional diversity or lag behind it?
- Link to: [[integrated information theory]]

**V. Regulatory implications**

- Standard regulation constrains functional diversity directly — bans instruments, caps leverage
- A reflexivity-scaling framework asks instead: what increases the system's capacity to model itself?
- Candidates: mandatory systemic risk disclosure, correlated stress testing, circuit breakers as forced self-observation moments
- Veloz's three perturbation types: state, process, structural — effective regulation operates at the structural level
- The goal is closing the gap between functional diversity scaling and reflexivity scaling
- Link to: [[Veloz]], [[regulatory levers]]

**VI. The deeper question**

- Soros' irreducibility claim vs your black box position: consciousness and reflexivity may be reducible in theory but not in practice — the generative process exists but we lack the tools
- Svatantrya reframed: not self-arising freedom as metaphysical brute fact, but the felt experience of a system whose self-model has become causally autonomous
- The garden itself as an attempt to increase the reflexivity of one mind — externalising the self-model so it can be observed, linked, and grown
- Link to: [[kala]], [[dissolving kanchukas]], [[essence of immanence]]
